    Experiential Learning in Innovation, Technology, and Entrepreneurship (ELITE) Program for Black Youth

    The Experiential Learning in Innovation, Technology, and Entrepreneurship (ELITE) Program for Black Youth has been designed as a practical solution to enable Black Youth to develop hands-on skills that will be immediately transferable to disruptive technologies, including robotics, automation, advanced manufacturing, and tissue engineering.

    This will be achieved by assembling teachers, engineers, scientists, and technologists at the University of Alberta, InnoTech Alberta, and industry partners to provide work-integrated training on real projects and relevant equipment. Candidate interns will be matched with hosts for full-time or part-time internships of up to 8 or 16 weeks. All interns will participate in leadership and entrepreneur training. The interns will receive counselling and coaching for resiliency and managing the demands of professional careers.

    Movements of Belonging Retreat

    This retreat is for you if you are a climate or social justice activist or grassroots organiser who is working with questions like:


    • What does it take to build resilient, authentic social change organisations that offer belonging to everyone committed to the vision?
    • What does it take to create a community of care that will support me and my colleagues to stay connected through challenges?
    • What does it feel like to be in a truly collaborative and welcoming space?
    • How do we counter cancel culture and other unhealthy ways we sometimes treat each other?
    • How do we build a movement that honours the dignity of all people?

    The retreat will be held between January 21 and 26, 2025 on Nex̱wlélex̱wm (Bowen Island). The aim of the gathering is to bring together between 15 to 20 climate, racial and social justice movement leaders from Greater Vancouver and southwest BC to experience the power of being in a community of belonging.

    Movements of Belonging will be facilitated by five experienced trainers and activists, Kazu Haga, Leonie Smith, HyoYoung Minna, Jeff Carolin, and catherine strickland. Each person brings a unique and varied set of skills, experiences and perspectives centred around building communities of belonging for nonviolent social change. This facilitation team, along with all participants, will co-create the gathering. Through experiential learning, ritual, play, creative expression and practices that build connection, we will raise our collective capacity for creating movements that offer belonging and meaning as we work towards a more just and healthy world.

    Necessary Trouble Collective: Community Care & Repair Sessions

    Hosted by the Necessary Trouble Collective and Building Belonging, Community Care & Repair (CC&R) is a series of bi-weekly online learning and practice sessions to build the skills and connections we need to create communities of belonging. The skills and practices offered are grounded in principles of nonviolence, emergence, Nonviolent Communication, and collective liberation. We intend them to be spaces for people working in social justice and climate justice movements and their intersections to gather, learn skills and receive care and support.

    Parathletes of Colour Digital Museum Launch Event

    November 30, 2024, from 2:00 PM to 5:00 PM at the McKernan Community League (11341 78 Ave NW, Edmonton)

    This event will showcase the untold stories of Canadian parathletes of colour, highlighting their achievements and contributions to sports. It is an excellent opportunity to unite as a community to celebrate diversity, inclusion, and the power of sports. We believe your presence at this event would significantly contribute to our goal of fostering awareness and promoting inclusivity.

    The Collective Action Forum: Sports for Inclusion, Connection, and Learning

    November 2, 2024, from 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM at the Mill Woods Senior Association (2619 Hewes Way SW, Edmonton)

    This event is a continuation of the important conversation we began at our last Forum on March 24, 2023, focusing on the barriers faced by racialized newcomer youth in sports participation. The Collective Action Forum is organized by AHC, a dynamic, community-based agency that provides settlement and capacity-building services to people of all ages and backgrounds. AHC works tirelessly to support underserved and marginalized communities in Edmonton and across Alberta, fostering an inclusive society by addressing the barriers equity-deserving groups face in fully participating in society.

    Objectives of this Forum are to:

    • Learn the results of the Newcomer Coach Mentorship research study as to the barriers of access to coaching in Edmonton
    • Learn about how others in the sport sector have connected with newcomer and cultural organizations
    • Engage in conversation with representatives of sport organizations, cultural communities, City Councilors, MLAs, and MPs on policy suggestions and recommendations to address barriers
    • Connect with cultural community organizations and youth leaders on their perspective on sport
